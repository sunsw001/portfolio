Best Before: A systematical way to solve unnoticeable problem

Overview

Context

According to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United States "Globally, one-third of the food produced for human consumption – approximately 1.3 billion tonnes – is lost or wasted each year." Food is lost or wasted at each stage of the Chinese food supply chain but, as with other middle-income countries, more food is beginning to be wasted in the consumption stage.

Food waste has a dual effect: on the one hand, consumers waste their hard-earned money, and on the other uneaten food contributes to the Greenhouse Effect.

Best Before is an APP aiming to solve the food waste problem. I hope that with the help of this APP, families will be more aware of food waste and thus control the release of methane, carbon dioxide to some extent.

What I did

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Contextual Inquiry
  • Prototyping
  • Usability Testing
  • UX/UI Design

Know our users

I interviewed 10 people with different habits of dealing with food. Some of them live alone, some of them live with their parents or kids and I tried to figure out their needs and pain points in food storage.

Possible Directions

  • How might we design a system which can help users input data easily?
  • How might we provide users with popular recipes to cook the food they don't want to eat?
  • How might we reduce users' burden to remember the expiration time?
